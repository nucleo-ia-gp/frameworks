# PMI Chapter Partnership Playbook

Reusable methodology for proposing a cooperation/partnership **with or through a PMI chapter**, grounded in
PMI's own public materials. Deal-specific applications stay in the private wiki; this page is the portable method.

## 1. Read the partner's STRATEGY, not just their product
Before pitching an ecosystem, read its strategy docs. For PMI:
- **PMI:Next** (Jan/2024 strategy). Purpose: "We maximize project success to elevate our world." Serves *current
  and aspiring project professionals*. **Where We Play (3 arenas):** (1) community-generated knowledge platforms
  & resources; (2) **career-long learning & development**; (3) "gold standard" professional certifications.
  Focus pillars include **PMIxAI**. Activation model: Innovate / Expand Globally / **Amplify across the PMI flywheel**.
- **M.O.R.E.** (2025 call to action). Reframes project success from the triple constraint (scope/budget/schedule)
  to **value perceived by stakeholders**: **M**anage Perceptions · **O**wn Project Success beyond PM success ·
  **R**elentlessly Reassess parameters · **E**xpand Perspective. "Community is our Superpower… tapping into the
  PMI flywheel." → use the partner's own words (flywheel, M.O.R.E., the 3 arenas) and they read you as fluent.

## 2. Map your offer to PMI's official partnership taxonomy
The **PMI Chapter Partnerships Framework** (Aug/2024) defines the vocabulary the chapter uses to approve a deal:
- **Definition:** a formal agreement (non-binding e.g. MOU, or binding e.g. contract) between a chapter and one or
  more external parties for a specific purpose. A vendor relationship is NOT a partnership. *PMI cannot give legal
  advice; chapters are separate legal entities and retain their own counsel* (never imply you provide legal templates).
- **Partner types:** Academic · Co-Branding · Content Distribution · Corporate Sponsorship · Government Agency ·
  Pro Bono · Research · Social & Community Impact. → label each front of your offer with its official type.
- **6 purposes:** Activate PMI:Next · Build Brand Awareness · Expand Outreach · Share Knowledge & Research ·
  Enhance Member Value · Strengthen Operations. → name the value exchange by these.
- **Process:** Need ID → Due Diligence → Partner Engagement → Additional DD & Evaluation → Internal Decision →
  Formalize (LOI → MOU → SOW → Partnership Agreement → NDA → IP/copyright → Comms Plan) → Monitor. *Note: a partner
  who proactively engages the chapter reverts to Step 1* (frame inbound interest as the start of the process).
- **RACI:** Responsible = Chapter Leader · Accountable = Chapter President · Consulted = other leaders + PMI Region
  Chapter Team · Informed = Chapter Leaders Board.

## 3. Score the REAL evaluation matrix (1-5, sum 6-30)
Criteria: (1) Strategic Alignment to PMI:Next · (2) Risk Exposure [5 = low risk] · (3) Partner Reach ·
(4a) Potential conversion (funnel) OR (4b) revenue/resources · (5) Financial investment by chapter [5 = low] ·
(6) Capacity investment by chapter [5 = low]. Winning partnerships score 5 on Strategic Alignment + Partner Reach +
low chapter investment; the recurring weak spot is criterion 4 → **make your conversion/funnel explicit**.

## 4. The single documented deal-breaker: competing certifications
Across PMI's own worked examples, the only rejected case was a partner that highlighted **competing
certifications** (conflict of interest → Risk Exposure). → Always frame your offer as **complementary, not
competing**: your training/audience *feeds into* PMI credentials, it does not substitute them.

## 5. Patterns that work in the pitch
- **Speak PMI:** label fronts by partner type; name the value exchange by the 6 purposes; tie the close to the
  "PMI flywheel"; connect the market problem to the M.O.R.E. value gap.
- **Roadmap, not list:** show breadth (e.g., a certification career roadmap) aligned to the "career-long learning"
  arena, with the on-theme item as the hero — breadth without dilution.
- **Precedent as proof:** PMI chapters already run these plays (Content Distribution, Academic with ATPs,
  Co-Branding with training orgs). Cite the *pattern* to show it is sanctioned, not experimental.
- **Low-friction entry, ambitious frame:** start informal (no signature), formalize via MOU then contract when
  value is proven; keep the narrative "build something big", not "start small".
